We understand the appeal of clicking "add to cart." It's fast, it's easy, and the price sometimes looks lower at first glance. But for a commercial piece of equipment that you'll rely on for 10+ years, buying local has advantages that are worth understanding before you decide.

## 1. You Can See and Test the Machine Before You Buy

A Ferris ISX2200 costs $15,000+. At that price point, spending 20 minutes at a dealership before you commit isn't just reasonable — it's smart. You can see how the controls feel, sit in the seat, get a sense of the visibility, and have a real conversation about whether that model matches your property.

None of that happens when you buy online.

## 2. Local Dealers Handle Warranty Work

Ferris warranty service has to be performed by an authorized dealer. If you buy from an online retailer or private seller and something goes wrong under warranty, you're driving to find a dealer anyway — but now you're dealing with a machine that wasn't set up or delivered by them, which creates friction.

When you buy from us, we're your service center from day one.

## 3. We Set the Machine Up Correctly

A new mower needs to be properly assembled, the deck needs to be leveled, and certain adjustments need to be made before first use. We do all of that before delivery. A crated machine shipped to your door doesn't come with that.

## 4. Financing Is Easier In Person

We work directly with top national lenders. You apply once, we handle the paperwork, and you drive away with your mower. Online purchases through third-party sites often don't include dealer financing options.

## 5. Ongoing Parts and Service Support

When your blades need sharpening, your belt snaps mid-season, or you need a filter in a hurry — we're 10 minutes down the road, not a 3-day shipping estimate away. We stock OEM Ferris parts and can source anything we don't have in stock.

## 6. We Know the Local Conditions

Mississippi terrain, grass types, and humidity are different from the Mid-Atlantic or Pacific Northwest. We know what setups work here, which deck options handle Bermuda and Zoysia best, and what issues tend to come up locally. That context matters.
