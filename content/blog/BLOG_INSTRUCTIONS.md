## Core Mission
Posts and pages should be **truly educational** about Ferris equipment. A reader who finishes a post should know more about a specific Ferris model, technology, use case, or buying decision than they did before. That earns trust, ranks for long-tail keywords, and turns into walk-ins.

Not advertising. Not vague hype. Not the same five marketing phrases on every page. Real information someone running a working operation can act on.

---

## Voice & Tone
Write like a person who works at the dealership and knows the equipment firsthand.

- Direct, practical, specific. Short sentences. Real words.
- Mississippi-specific context where relevant: heat, humidity, Bermuda grass, long mowing seasons, hurricane prep, Pine Belt soils.
- Mention Dykes Motors naturally once or twice — not in every paragraph.
- Sound like someone who sells and services these machines every day.
- No filler sentences. No corporate language. No robotic transitions ("In conclusion", "In this article we will explore").

### Banned phrases / patterns (kill on sight)
- "Genuine" / "Premium" / "Superior" stacked on adjectives
- "Designed to" / "Engineered for" / "Built to deliver"
- Em-dashes used as dramatic pauses (single sentence em-dashes for clauses are fine; "It's a beast — and it's reliable" is not)
- "It's not just X, it's Y" — banned entirely
- "In today's world" / "In the world of" openers
- Stock corporate intros and outros

### Hard content rules
1. **No fabricated Casey quotes.** Don't invent things Casey said.
2. **No time-based claims.** dykespower.com opened in 2025. Don't write "spent years," "decades of experience," "years in the business," etc.
3. **No lender names.** Use "top national lenders" or "as low as 4.9% APR for qualified credit." Never name Synchrony, Sheffield, Ally, etc.
4. **No credit-challenged messaging** anywhere. Qualified buyers only.
5. **One published piece per day, max** (drip rule). Never bulk-dump.
6. **Mobile-first writing.** Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences). Real line breaks.
7. **dykespower.com only.** Never touch dykesmotors.com or car content from here.

### Image rules
- AVOID `/images/ferris/lifestyle-landscaper-1.webp` (the stand-on-operator hero shot — owner rejected it twice).
- Use real images from `/public/images/ferris/`.
- Frontmatter `image` field required.

## Educational Depth Standard
Each post should answer at least three of these for the reader:

1. What specific Ferris model(s) fit this use case, and which deck size + engine combo?
2. What does the technology actually do (suspension, iCD+, Oil Guard, EFI, hydro-gear transaxle)?
3. What does it cost? (MSRP from /catalog or model page is fine. No fake pricing.)
4. What real productivity number applies — acres/hour, hours/year, etc.?
5. What's the maintenance reality — service intervals, parts cost, common wear items?
6. How does it compare to the Toro/Scag/Kubota/Bad Boy alternative the buyer is also considering? (Honest, no trash-talking.)

If a post doesn't teach the reader anything specific about Ferris equipment, it's wrong.

---

## Post Format
Vary the format based on the topic. Don't use the same structure every day.

- **How-to guide** — numbered steps, practical and actionable
- **Comparison** — side-by-side, honest pros and cons
- **Advice piece** — conversational, like talking to a customer on the lot
- **Industry-specific deep-dive** — written directly to that audience's world (chicken farmers, golf supers, landscapers)

**Length: 500–850 words.** Some posts should be short and punchy; some deeper. Don't pad.

Use subheadings naturally — not as a rigid template. 3–5 H2 sections is typical.

### Landing-page hard rules
- Real geographic facts only. Look up real driving distance and routes. No invented landmarks.
- Same voice rules as blog posts.
- Add the new route to `app/sitemap.ts` (or whatever sitemap mechanism is in use — read it first).
- Confirm there's no existing page for this city before building.
- One landing page per Tuesday. Don't build multiple in one commit.

### Companion blog post (Wednesday)
The Wednesday blog post should reference the city built Tuesday — link to `/locations/{city}` from a relevant content angle (e.g., "Ferris Service in {City}, MS" or "Why {City} Cattle Farmers Are Running Commercial Zero Turns"). This creates the internal link Google needs to crawl the new landing page.
