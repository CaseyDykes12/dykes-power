## The Ferris 500S — Built for the Crossover Buyer

The 500S sits right at the line between residential and commercial. It's a real commercial frame and deck with Ferris's ForeFront Suspension Technology — not the full independent suspension you get on the ISX models, but a genuine suspension system that makes a huge difference compared to rigid-frame mowers.

**What you get:**
- 25 HP Briggs & Stratton CXi engine
- Dual Hydro-Gear transaxles
- 48", 52", or 61" fabricated deck options
- Up to 8.5 mph ground speed
- Starting at $6,799

The 500S is the right call for homeowners with 3–10 acres, hobby farms in Smith County, or anyone who wants commercial durability without the commercial price tag. If your terrain is mostly flat to moderate, this mower handles it without issue.

## The Ferris ISX800 — When You Need More Machine

The ISX800 is a step up in every way that matters for demanding properties. The suspension upgrades to Ferris's full system, the transaxles jump to Hydro-Gear ZT-3400s, and the top speed hits 10 mph. This is the mower landscaping crews in the Pine Belt rely on to run 40+ hours a week.

**What you get:**
- 24–25 HP engine options (Kawasaki FT730V or Briggs & Stratton CXi)
- Hydro-Gear ZT-3400 transaxles (heavier duty than the 500S)
- 52" or 60" fabricated deck with iCD cutting system
- Up to 10 mph ground speed
- Starting at $10,699

If you're running a crew, mowing commercial properties around Seminary or Taylorsville, or your property has rough, uneven terrain — the ISX800 earns its price difference every time you sit on it.

## So Which One?

**Go with the 500S if:** You're a homeowner mowing 3–10 acres, your ground is relatively flat, and you want a mower that'll last 10+ years without paying for features you don't need.

**Go with the ISX800 if:** You're mowing commercially, your terrain punishes rigid mowers, or you want the most productive machine you can run. The upgraded drive system and [full independent suspension](/blog/ferris-independent-suspension-explained) pay for themselves in ride quality, speed, and longevity.

Both are real commercial machines built to last in Mississippi heat and humidity. The difference comes down to how hard you're going to push it. If you're not sure, [come see them side by side](/contact) — that usually settles it in about five minutes.
