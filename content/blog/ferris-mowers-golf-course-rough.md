## Why Rough Mowing Is Different

Rough is where the mower gets abused. You're cutting at 2–4 inches, often over ground that isn't perfectly level, hitting tree roots, sprinkler heads, and everything golfers drop in the rough. The deck takes more impact than fairway cuts. The engine runs hotter because it's pushing through thicker, wetter grass — especially early morning after overnight humidity rolls in from the south.

Mississippi summers compound this. In July and August, Bermuda rough grows aggressively. You may be cutting the same stretch twice a week to keep heights consistent. A mower that can't handle that pace without mechanical issues costs you more than you realize — we wrote about [the real cost of equipment downtime](/blog/real-cost-of-mower-downtime-landscaping-mississippi) for commercial operators, and for a golf course, losing your rough mower mid-round isn't just an inconvenience. It's a liability.

## What Specs to Look For

**Deck width.** For rough management on a full-sized course, 61 or 72-inch decks are standard. A 61-inch gives you more maneuverability around bunker surrounds and tight transitions to the fairway. A 72 opens up rough cutting time significantly on larger properties.

**Suspension.** Golf course terrain isn't flat. Even well-maintained courses have uneven areas in the rough, particularly around rough-cut banks, mounds, and natural areas. Ferris's independent suspension system keeps the deck in contact with the ground through elevation changes — that means consistent cut height even on rough terrain. We go deeper on [how the Ferris suspension system works](/blog/ferris-independent-suspension-explained) if you want the mechanical breakdown. The short version: your operator is less beat up at the end of the day, and the cut stays more uniform on ground that isn't.

**Engine reliability.** You're not doing casual mowing. A Kawasaki or Briggs & Stratton Commercial Turf engine in the 25–37 hp range handles Mississippi rough through a full season without laboring. That's the range where the Ferris ISX 800 and IS 3200Z operate, and it's not an accident.
