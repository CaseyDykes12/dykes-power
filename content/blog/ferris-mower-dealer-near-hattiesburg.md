## Question 3: "Which Ferris Should I Actually Look At?"

This is the one that matters. Short answer — depends on what you're mowing.

**Home property, 2–5 acres:** The Ferris 500S hits the sweet spot. Enough power for Mississippi grass, a deck size (52" or 61") that finishes in reasonable time, and it's priced so the monthly payment isn't crazy. We sell more of these to homeowners than anything else.

**Home property, 5–15 acres, or rough terrain:** Step up to the [ISX2200](/catalog). Ferris invented the independent suspension mower, and this is the full system. If you're on hills, uneven ground, or anything with washouts — the suspension takes the beating out of mowing. Your back will thank you.

**Commercial landscape crew:** Depends on your route. City-lot crews in Hattiesburg mostly run [stand-ons](/catalog?category=Stand-On+Mowers) — smaller footprint, fit on a trailer with a zero-turn, and they're faster getting on and off between yards. Larger-property crews run the bigger [zero-turns](/catalog) — IS700Z or ISX2200.

**Farm or pasture mowing:** We wrote a separate piece on [zero turns for Mississippi farms](/blog/zero-turn-mowers-for-mississippi-farms) — the short version is you want something with fabricated deck construction and 25+ HP. The 500S is fine for fence lines and smaller pastures. The ISX2200 or IS700Z for anything serious.

And we're not going to tell you the mower will last forever without maintenance. Commercial mowers need oil changes, blade sharpening, air filter changes, belt inspections. That's part of owning one. What you don't want is a residential mower that lasts 200 hours before something expensive fails. Commercial Ferris machines go 2,000+ hours with basic care.
