If you're mowing 5 acres in Mississippi, you need something that can handle heat, humidity, thick Bermuda or Zoysia turf, and uneven terrain without breaking down every other season. Here's what we recommend.

## Why 5 Acres Changes the Equation

At 5 acres, you're past the consumer mower range. You need a commercial-grade machine — not because you're a lawn professional, but because commercial mowers are built to handle the runtime and conditions that will chew through a residential unit in a couple of seasons.

Plan on 1.5 to 2.5 hours of mowing per session depending on your deck width and terrain. That kind of runtime demands a quality engine, a real hydrostatic drive, and a deck that holds up to Mississippi summers.

## What to Avoid

- **Big box store mowers** — Husqvarna and Cub Cadet units sold at Lowe's and Home Depot are built to a price point, not for 5-acre runtime
- **Underpowered engines** — Anything under 22 HP will struggle with thick Southern turf
- **Smaller decks** — On 5 acres, a 42" deck will cost you an extra 30–45 minutes per session. Go 52" minimum

## Deck Size Recommendation for 5 Acres

A 52" deck is the sweet spot for most 5-acre Mississippi properties — wide enough to cover ground fast, but maneuverable enough for tighter spaces near fences or landscaping.
